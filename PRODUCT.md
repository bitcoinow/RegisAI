# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Compliance officers (CCOs) and legal/risk staff at financial firms: RIAs, fintechs, insurance carriers, and banks. They work in high-stakes regulatory environments where a missed gap can mean a fine or licence risk. They open this tool with a specific job: find out where their compliance manual falls short before a regulator does.

## Product Purpose

Regis is an AI-powered compliance gap analysis tool. Users upload a PDF compliance manual; the app extracts the text, runs a structured gap analysis against jurisdiction-specific regulatory requirements (US, EU, UK), and returns a prioritised findings report with risk ratings and remediation actions. It replaces days of manual review with a focused, actionable report.

## Brand Personality

Professional, modern, approachable. Confident without being cold. The tone of a senior compliance counsel who also happens to be a clear communicator — not a consultant trying to impress, not a startup trying to seem fun.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS: purple gradients, growth-speak, "supercharge your compliance workflow"
- Big 4 consulting: dense grey layouts, KPMG/Deloitte corporate weight
- LegalTech startups: sterile white + navy, rows of trust badges, performative credibility signals
- Bloomberg / financial terminals: dark dense data grids, information overload as a feature

## Design Principles

1. **Clarity over decoration.** Every element should make the product easier to understand, not just prettier to look at.
2. **Institutional confidence.** The visual language should feel like it belongs in a boardroom, not a startup pitch deck.
3. **Show the work.** Trust is earned through transparency — surface the methodology, the requirements, the reasoning.
4. **Precision at every scale.** From typography to risk badges, details signal competence.
5. **Approachable rigour.** Serious doesn't mean intimidating. Compliance is complex; the UI should reduce that complexity, not mirror it.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA minimum. Body text must hit ≥4.5:1 contrast. Financial professionals may use this in varied lighting conditions (office, travel). No motion dependencies for core functionality.
